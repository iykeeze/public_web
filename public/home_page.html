Welcome to the Public Web

Introduction

The concept of the public web is a cornerstone of the internet, representing the open and accessible nature of online information. This ubiquitous web, available to anyone with a web browser, democratizes access to knowledge and resources. Unlike gated content that requires logins or subscriptions, the public web allows unrestricted exploration, fostering an environment of free information exchange.

The Evolution of the Public Web Early Days of the Internet

The origins of the public web trace back to the early days of the internet when Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web in 1989. Initially, the web was a collection of static pages accessible to anyone with an internet connection. This era was characterized by a pioneering spirit of openness and sharing, laying the foundation for the expansive web we know today.

Growth and Expansion

As technology advanced, so did the capabilities of the public web. The introduction of search engines like Google, Yahoo, and Bing revolutionized how people accessed information. The web grew exponentially, encompassing diverse content ranging from academic research to entertainment, news, and personal blogs.
